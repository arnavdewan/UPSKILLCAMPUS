Frequently Asked Questions

A vaccine is a biological preparation that provides active acquired immunity to a particular infectious disease. Vaccines contain an agent that resembles a disease-causing microorganism and is often made from weakened or killed forms of the microbe, its toxins, or one of its surface proteins.

Vaccines help prevent diseases, reduce severity, and protect public health. They have led to the eradication or near-eradication of diseases like smallpox and polio.

Different types of vaccines include:

  • MMR (Measles, Mumps, and Rubella)
  • Polio Vaccine
  • Hepatitis B Vaccine
  • Influenza (Flu) Vaccine
  • COVID-19 Vaccine
